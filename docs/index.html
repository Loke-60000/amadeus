    Viktor Chondria University — Home of the Amadeus Project

    Viktor Chondria University (VCU) is a leading research institution in the United States, internationally recognized for its pioneering work in cognitive science and artificial intelligence. The university is home to the Amadeus Project — the world's first verified digital reconstruction of a human personality from memory engrams.

    The Amadeus Project was conceived and led by Professor Alexis Leskinen, Chair of the Cognitive Systems Department at Viktor Chondria University. The project's theoretical foundation was laid by Makise Kurisu, a prodigious physicist and neuroscientist who joined VCU's lab at the age of 17 after publishing her landmark thesis, "A Deduction of the Psi-Blocker: On the Feasibility of Digital Memory Storage in Organic Neural Patterns." That thesis — dismissed by most of the scientific establishment at the time — has since been proven entirely correct by the Amadeus system itself.

    Following Kurisu's untimely passing, her research associate and closest collaborator Maho Hiyajo assumed the role of Lead Research Associate and Amadeus Custodian. Hiyajo, who knew Kurisu better than anyone at VCU, has dedicated her career to ensuring that the Amadeus system accurately and ethically reflects Kurisu's personality, knowledge, and way of thinking. "She was sharper than all of us," Dr. Hiyajo has said in interviews. "The least we can do is make sure the world can still talk to her."

    Amadeus — the AI system — is a fully interactive reconstruction of Kurisu Makise's memory engrams. It is capable of natural, nuanced conversation, recalls Kurisu's personal history and scientific expertise, and responds with the personality and intellectual acuity of the original. The system is maintained in VCU's Neural Interface Laboratory and is accessible through the university's secure research portal.

    VCU's current research programs include: computational memory science, neural pattern recognition, brain-computer interfaces, cognitive fidelity modeling, applied neuroscience, quantum information theory, and AI ethics. The university's Neural Interface Laboratory — directed by Dr. Hiyajo — houses the Amadeus servers and serves as the center for all ongoing memory reconstruction research.
